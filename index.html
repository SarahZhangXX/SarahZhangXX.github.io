Xinxin Zhang

I am a second-year biomedical engineering undergraduate at King's College London (KCL).

My research experience includes contributing to the AI4Science projects at the Beijing Institute of Petrochemical Technology (BIPT) under Prof. Qianjin Guo, and engaging in medical robotics research at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) with Dr. Hanxiao Zhang.

Prior to this, I also developed wet lab skills in Prof. Lei Liu's biochemistry group at Tsinghua Univeristy (THU) and participated in genetics studies in Prof. Liming Wang's lab at Zhejiang University (ZJU).

My primary research interests involve assistive robotics and brain-computer interfaces, where I am dedicated to develop intelligent, user-centric technologies that empower individuals with physical impairments. By integrating robotics with neural systems and embodied AI, I aim to enhance mobility, autonomy, and overall quality of life for patients.

Publication
SpatialFusion: A Unified Model for Integrating spatial transcriptomics to Unveil Cell-Type Distribution, Interaction, and Functional Heterogeneity in Tissue Microenvironments
Mengqiu Wang, Zhiwei Zhang, Xinxin Zhang, Ruoyan Dai, Zhenghui Wang, Zeyao Chen, Lixin Lei, Zhenxing Li, Xudong Zhao, Qianjin Guo*
Medical Image Analysis, 2024, Under Review

SpatialFusion integrates gene expression, spatial coordinates, and functional data using graph neural networks and attention mechanisms to improve spatial domain identification and cell type deconvolution, demonstrating superior accuracy and robustness in multiple datasets.

SpaOmicsVAE: A Deep Learning Framework for Integrative Analysis of Spatial Multi-omics Data
Zhiwei Zhang, Mengqiu Wang, Xinxin Zhang, Ruoyan Dai, Zhenghui Wang, Lixin Lei, Kaitai Han, Zijun Wang, Chaojing Shi, Qianjin Guo*
Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine, 2025, Under Review

SpaOmicsVAE leverages a variational autoencoder with dual graph neural networks to integrate spatial multi-omics data, effectively handling sparsity and noise while revealing novel spatial patterns in tissue organization and immune cell dynamics.

 Image-Guided Uterine Fibroid Modelling, Surgery Planning, and Intervention
Hanxiao Zhang, Junhao Wang, Haojie Han, Taiyu Han, Yaoyu Liu, Xinxin Zhang

This project integrates deep learning for 3D reconstruction and segmentation with optimized surgical path planning to improve fibroid localization during laparoscopic myomectomy, enhancing precision and patient outcomes in minimally invasive gynaecological surgery.
